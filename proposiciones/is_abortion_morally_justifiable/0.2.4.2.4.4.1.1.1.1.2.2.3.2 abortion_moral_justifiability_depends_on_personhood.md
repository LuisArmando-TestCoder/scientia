## argument_reasons

- The assertion assumes that moral rights, specifically the right to life, are exclusive to 'persons' rather than all biological members of a species, necessitating a definition of what constitutes a person (Lockean vs. Biological accounts).
- It implies that if personhood is absent, the moral weight of the fetus does not override bodily autonomy, whereas if it is present, the conflict moves to a prioritisation of competing rights (Tooley vs. Thomson).
