## reconclusion

The proposition attempts to force a collapse of the moral justification of abortion by making the ontological status of the fetus and the adult identical; while this creates a consistent internal logic, it fails to achieve external empiricism because it ignores the unique biological dependency of the fetus on the maternal host, which creates a conflict of rights unique to this context. and for all this, the claim of identical moral rights is an axiomatic decision, not a logical necessity, leaving the justification of abortion as a conflict between the right to exist and the right to self-determination.
