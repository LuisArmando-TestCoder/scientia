## argument_reasons

- The core argument posits that the biological event of conception (formation of a unique zygote) is functionally equivalent to the attainment of 'personhood', which carries a full suite of moral rights.
- The secondary premise links the moral permissibility of abortion directly to the personhood status of the fetus, implying that if personhood is present, termination is a moral violation of the right to life.
- A tertiary assumption exists that personhood is a binary state (existent or non-existent) rather than a scalar or emergent property.

## conclusion

If the biological start of life is equated with moral personhood, then any termination is equivalent to homicide; however, because personhood requires functional or relational attributes not present at conception and bodily autonomy presents a competing right, the premise is logically fragile. And for all this, the statement that personhood begins at conception is a categorical error that confuses biological existence with moral status.
