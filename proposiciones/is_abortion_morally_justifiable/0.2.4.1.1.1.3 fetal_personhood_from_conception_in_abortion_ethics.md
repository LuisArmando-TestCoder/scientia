## conclusion

If we define personhood as the presence of unique human DNA, then a fetus is a person, but because personhood in ethical literature (e.g., Singer, Tooley) often requires cognitive agency or sentience which a zygote lacks, the moral justifiability of abortion depends on which incompatible axiom one prioritizes, and for all this, fetal personhood is an unfalsifiable metaphysical claim rather than a biological fact.

## reconclusion

The statement 'A fetus is a person from conception' is a definitional stake in the ground; if one accepts the axiom of potentiality, the moral conclusion is total, yet if one demands evidence of sentience, the statement is false, making the debate a clash of foundational categories rather than a disagreement over data.

## preconceptual_reconclusion

Conception creates unique DNA -> Unique DNA defines a new biological organism -> If moral personhood is tied to biological organism status then the fetus is a person -> If the fetus is a person then it has a right to life -> If the right to life is absolute then abortion is not justifiable -> However if personhood requires sentience then the zygote is not a person -> If the zygote is not a person then its rights do not override the mother's autonomy -> Since the definition of personhood is an unsettled philosophical axiom the absolute truth value remains undefined.
