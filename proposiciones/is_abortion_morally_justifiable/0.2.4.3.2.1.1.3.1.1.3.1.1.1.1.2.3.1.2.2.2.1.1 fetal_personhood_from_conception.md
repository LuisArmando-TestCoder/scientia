- Ontological continuity: Life begins at conception, thus personhood must be a continuous property from that point.
- Potentiality: The zygote contains the blueprint and capacity for adult personhood, requiring only time and environment.
- Moral Equivalence: If a fetus is a person, it possesses an inherent right to life that supersedes bodily autonomy (context-dependent).

## logical_tension

- **paradox**: The Potentiality Paradox: An entity is treated as having the rights of what it *will* be, but those rights are used to ensure it *can* become that thing.
- **ambiguity**: The term 'person' shifts between biological ('human life') and moral/legal ('bearer of rights').
- **useful_contradiction**: An embryo is genetically 'human' but phenomenologically 'absent' of the traits typically associated with personhood (consciousness/suffering).
