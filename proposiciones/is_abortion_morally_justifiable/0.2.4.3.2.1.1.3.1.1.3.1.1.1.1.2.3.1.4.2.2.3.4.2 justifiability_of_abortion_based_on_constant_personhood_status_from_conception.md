## conclusion

If personhood is defined as a constant starting at conception, then the fetus possesses an absolute right to life equivalent to an adult, and for all this abortion becomes a violation of that right unless a higher-order principle of bodily self-ownership is invoked to negate the moral obligation to sustain another. and for all this the justified nature of abortion is logically incompatible with the axiom of constant, equal personhood.
