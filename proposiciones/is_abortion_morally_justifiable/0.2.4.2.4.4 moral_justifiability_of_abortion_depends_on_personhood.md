## conclusion

## preconceptual_reconclusion

We are trying to use a rule that hasn't been finished yet. Until we agree on what makes a person a 'person', the rule can't tell us what is right or wrong.

## causal_chain

Abortion involves an entity with biological life. Moral justifiability requires a rights-based framework. Rights are traditionally assigned to 'persons'. If the entity is a person, it has a right to life. However, a person's right to life does not automatically grant a right to use another person's body. Therefore, justifiability must look at both the personhood of the fetus and the bodily autonomy of the pregnant person. Because personhood is defined differently by different people based on heartbeats, brains, or souls, the starting point of the argument is always changing. If the starting point changes, the final answer changes. Thus, the argument that it depends *only* on personhood is a simplified bridge that collapses under the weight of competing rights and the lack of a universal definition of the 'person' variable.
