Equivalence of Moral Status Across Biological Development

- Biological continuity: The claim that there is no discrete point where 'non-human' becomes 'human' after conception.

- Conflict of rights: The juxtaposition of fetal right to life against adult bodily autonomy.

## conclusion

If we define personhood by DNA, then a fetus is a person, but if we define it by consciousness, it is not; this conflict remains unresolved because moral rights are social constructs rather than biological constants, and for all this, the equality of rights is a matter of chosen axioms rather than discovered facts. and for all this, the proposition remains an uncollapsable moral choice.

## reconclusion

Moral rights are not inherent properties of biological tissue but are assigned by moral communities; therefore, equating a fetus to an adult requires an axiomatic leap that overrides developmental and autonomous distinctions. and for all this, the equivalence is an ethical imposition rather than a logical necessity.
