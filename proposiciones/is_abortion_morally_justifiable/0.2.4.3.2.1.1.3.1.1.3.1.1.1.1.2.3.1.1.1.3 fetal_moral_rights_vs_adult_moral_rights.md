## causal_chain

A fetus contains the unique genetic code of a human being, which leads some to define it as a person from the first moment of existence. If it is a person, it must logically possess the same fundamental rights as any other person, such as an adult. This creates a moral parity where ending the life of a fetus is equivalent to ending the life of an adult. However, this definition of personhood is an assigned status rather than a physical property. Because it is assigned, different people assign it at different times based on viability, birth, or consciousness. This discrepancy means the original statement cannot be proven true without first proving the unprovable definition of when a 'someone' begins. Therefore, the moral rights of a fetus remain a site of social and legal negotiation rather than a fixed biological fact, and this negotiation determines whether abortion is viewed as a right or a crime.
