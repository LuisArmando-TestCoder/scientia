## causal_chain

An organism begins at conception with a unique genetic code which establishes biological humanity but not necessarily social personhood because personhood is a set of relationships and capacities recognized by a community that includes the ability to exist independently. If we assume personhood exists at conception then the fetus is granted a right to life which in any other person-to-person context does not include the right to occupy or utilize the organs of another person without their continuous consent. Since an adult does not have the right to force another adult to provide organ or blood support even to prevent death it follows that granting this right to a fetus makes fetal rights greater than or different from adult rights. This difference breaks the definition of 'identical' because the rights are now asymmetric in their demands on others. Therefore the claim of identical rights is a logical error because it ignores the physical reality of gestation which creates a unique category of interaction that cannot be mapped onto the standard model of independent adult rights. Consequently the moral justifiability of abortion cannot be dismissed by a claim of rights-identity as the identity itself is a fiction that collapses when applied to the actual physical requirements of fetal development which leads to the inevitable conclusion that rights are contextual and developmental rather than static and identical from conception.
