## reconclusion

The justification of abortion rests on the 'Functionalist' axiom that moral status is earned through the manifestation of consciousness; while biologically human, the early fetus lacks the cognitive architecture to anchor 'interests', therefore rendering the act of abortion a non-moral event regarding the fetus itself until the point of sentience.

## preconceptual_reconclusion

It's like waiting for a computer to turn on. Before the screen is bright and the programs start, it's just a box. It's only a computer you can play with once the power is on.

## causal_chain

Biological life begins at conception but moral personhood is a subset of life defined by internal experience. Internal experience is a product of brain complexity. Brain complexity develops over time. Therefore, early-stage fetuses lack the hardware for experience. If there is no experience, there is no 'someone' to be harmed. If there is no 'someone' to be harmed, the moral weight shifts entirely to the existing person carrying it. Thus, abortion is justifiable before the 'light' of the mind turns on because you cannot hurt a mind that does not yet exist.
