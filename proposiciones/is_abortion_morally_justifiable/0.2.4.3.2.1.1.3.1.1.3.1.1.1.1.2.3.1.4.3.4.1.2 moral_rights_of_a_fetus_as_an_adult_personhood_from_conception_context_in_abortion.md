## reconclusion

The assertion of fetal-adult rights equivalence is a normative axiom, not a deductive fact; it requires the injection of non-empirical personhood definitions to overcome the developmental gap between a zygote and a sentient human. Consequently, the justification remains rooted in subjective moral frameworks (theological or essentialist) rather than universal logical reduction.

## causal_chain

A human life begins as a biological event at conception which fixes a unique genetic identity that serves as the potential blueprint for a complete human person. If this potential is assigned a status of 'actual personhood', then the fetus necessarily inherits the full spectrum of adult moral rights including the right to life. However, since actual personhood in a social and cognitive sense requires a nervous system capable of perception and interaction which a zygote lacks, the claim of equality depends on an external metaphysical axiom rather than biological parity. Since this axiom is not universally shared or empirically verifiable, the moral rights remain in a state of superposition. If the axiom is true abortion is homicide; if the axiom is false abortion is a medical procedure. Because the system cannot collapse this without choosing a side, the initial proposition functions as a boundary-marker for a belief system rather than a universal truth, thereby justifying the conclusion that the claim is an ontological projection of values onto a biological process.
