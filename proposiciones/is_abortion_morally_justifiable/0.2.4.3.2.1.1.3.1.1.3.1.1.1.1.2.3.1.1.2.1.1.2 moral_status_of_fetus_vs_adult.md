## causal_chain

Conception initiates a unique genetic sequence which establishes the biological foundation of a human life. This biological foundation is used to claim personhood status. If personhood status is granted, then moral rights are logically conferred to protect that person. If moral rights are conferred, they must be equal to avoid arbitrary discrimination. However, the fetus resides within another person who also possesses absolute moral rights. These two sets of rights occupy the same physical space. If rights are equal, neither can supersede the other without a violation. Therefore, the claim of equal rights either stops the possibility of choice or requires the denial of the host's rights. Because the host's rights are already established, the introduction of equal fetal rights creates a structural contradiction in law and ethics that can only be resolved by choosing which rights-holder is the primary 'node' of existence, which is a choice that cannot be made by logic alone but by the starting definitions of the system.
