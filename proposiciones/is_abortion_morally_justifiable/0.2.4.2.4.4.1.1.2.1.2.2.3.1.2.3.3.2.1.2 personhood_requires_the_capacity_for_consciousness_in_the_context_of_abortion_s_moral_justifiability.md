## causal_chain

Moral justifiability is defined as the adherence to rules protecting persons. Personhood is defined by the unique property of the capacity for consciousness. The capacity for consciousness requires a matured central nervous system. A fetus in early development lacks a matured central nervous system. Lacking this system, it lacks the capacity for consciousness. Lacking the capacity for consciousness, it fails the requirement for personhood. Lacking personhood, it lacks the moral status of a rights-bearing entity. Without such status, its termination does not violate the moral rules protecting persons. Therefore, under these specific definitions, the act is justifiable because the object of the act does not trigger the protective classification.
