## conclusion

If we define a fetus as an equivalent person, then the logic follows that it possesses a right to life that competes with the mother's autonomy, yet because the 'Right to Life' does not inherently grant the right to use another's organs, the syllogism remains incomplete without a secondary axiom regarding bodily obligations, and for all this abortion can only be declared 'unjustifiable' if one axiomatically prioritizes biological life over individual liberty in all circumstances.
