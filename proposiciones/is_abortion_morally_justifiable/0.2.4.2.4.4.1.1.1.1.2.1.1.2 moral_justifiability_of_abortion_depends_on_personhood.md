## causal_chain

Abortion involves two biological entities in a shared physical space. The prompt asserts that only the status of the fetus (personhood) determines moral justifiability. Moral justifiability is defined as the alignment of an action with ethical imperatives. One common ethical imperative is the Right to Life (often tied to personhood). Another common ethical imperative is Bodily Autonomy (the right to control one's own physical self). If personhood is the 'sole' factor, then Bodily Autonomy must have zero weight in the calculation. Since Bodily Autonomy is a foundational principle in medical ethics (Informed Consent, Right to Refuse Treatment), it cannot be assigned zero weight without a specific, justified axiom that does not exist in the prompt's context. Therefore, the mono-causal link between personhood and justifiability is broken by the presence of a second, independent moral variable. This renders the 'solely' claim logically invalid. Consequently, morality in this context must be a multi-variable function. The original proposition collapses under the weight of its own restrictive scope.
