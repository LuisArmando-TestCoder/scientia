## causal_chain

Determining moral rights requires a definition of the subject who holds them. If we define the subject purely by its genetic code (Axiom: Biology = Person), then the moment that code is complete (Conception) marks the start of the subject. If the subject is the start of the person, then all rules protecting persons (Universal Human Rights) must apply immediately and fully. However, if rights are linked to the ability to feel, think, or exist as an individual (Relational/Cognitive Axiom), then a fetus does not yet meet the criteria for adult status. This conflict between 'Inherent Value' and 'Emergent Value' means that the initial premise contains within it its own conclusion. Therefore, the validity of fetal rights is a loop where the answer you get depends entirely on the door you choose to walk through before you even start the math. Thus, any claim of 'same rights' is an anchored belief rather than a derived proof from neutral first principles.
