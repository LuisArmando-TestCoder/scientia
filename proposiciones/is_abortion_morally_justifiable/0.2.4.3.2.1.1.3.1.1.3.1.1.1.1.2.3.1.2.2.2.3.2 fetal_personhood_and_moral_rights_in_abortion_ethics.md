## input_semantic_node

Fetal personhood and moral rights in abortion ethics

## central_semantic_node

Fetal-Adult Moral Equivalence

## argument_reasons

- Identity Continuity Argument: The claim that a fetus is a person from conception relies on the biological continuity of the organism.
- Moral Rights Parity: If personhood is granted at conception, then moral rights (such as the right to life) are argued to be equivalent to those of an adult.
- Justification via Personhood: The moral status of abortion is directly modulated by the ontological status assigned to the fetus.

## ontological_signature

- **nature**: Axiomatic moral proposition
- **function**: To establish a legal and ethical prohibition of abortion by equating developmental stages.
- **domain**: Bioethics, Legal Philosophy, Ontology
- **form**: Syllogistic dependency
- **tension**: Conflicts with bodily autonomy and the 'gradualist' view of consciousness.
- **limit**: Dependent on the definition of 'person', which is not a biological fact but a social/metaphysical construct.

## boolean_formula_to_natural_language

If a fetus being a person (B) implies it having adult rights (A), then the moral status of abortion (C) depends on whether those rights are absolute or relative to the mother's autonomy.

## conclusion

If personhood is defined biologically at conception then adult rights follow, but since personhood is a philosophical construct and not a biological fact, the equivalence is an unproven axiom; and for all this fetal-adult moral parity is a subjective categorical assignment rather than a logical necessity.

## logical_tension

- **paradox**: The Potentiality Paradox: A thing cannot be identical to what it has the potential to become while it is still becoming it.
- **ambiguity**: The term 'person' shifts between 'biological human' and 'moral agent'.
- **useful_contradiction**: A fetus is both 'part of the mother' and a 'distinct developing entity', making it a biological bridge that defies simple categorization.

## global_reevaluation

## reconclusion

The proposition that a fetus has the same moral rights as an adult is a metaphysical postulate that serves as a circular foundation for anti-abortion ethics, failing to achieve empirical collapse due to the lack of an objective, non-arbitrary definition of 'personhood'.
