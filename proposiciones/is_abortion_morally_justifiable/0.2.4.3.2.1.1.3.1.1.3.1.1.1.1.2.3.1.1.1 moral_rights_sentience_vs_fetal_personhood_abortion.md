## conclusion

If rights require sentience as a prerequisite, then a non-sentient fetus cannot possess moral rights regardless of its biological classification as a person, and since sentience is absent in early gestation, the claim of equivalent adult rights is logically void in that period, and for all this abortion remains a conflict between the actual rights of a sentient host and the potential/future rights of a developing organism. and for all this the moral justifiability of abortion depends entirely on whether one prioritizes functional capacity (sentience) or biological potentiality (personhood).

## global_reevaluation

- **state**: self-referential
- **criteria**: The arguments are circular: personhood is defined to grant rights, and rights are only granted to those defined as persons. The premises 'load' the conclusion before the analysis begins.

## reconclusion

The conflict is not empirical but axiomatic; by reduction, if sentience is the ground for rights, the fetus lacks them initially. If conception is the ground, rights are granted regardless of capacity. Because the input mandates both a sentience requirement and a fetal-rights claim, it is a self-nullifying logical structure unless one premise is discarded. and for all this the justifiability of the act is an output of the chosen starting axiom, not an independent truth.

## preconceptual_reconclusion

The two rules are fighting each other. You have to pick which rule is more important: 'being able to feel' or 'just being there from the start.' You can't follow both at the same time and get one answer.

## causal_chain

Conception initiates a biological trajectory of a human organism. Personhood is an assigned moral status often linked to this trajectory. Sentience is an emergent property of that organism's development. If rights are anchored to sentience then a fetus without a brain cannot have rights. If rights are anchored to conception then sentience is irrelevant. The assertion that a fetus has adult rights while also asserting rights require sentience is a logical contradiction because a fetus is not sentient at conception. Therefore abortion's moral status is a direct function of which axiom is prioritized. If sentience is the gatekeeper then abortion is justifiable before the gate opens. If personhood starts at conception then the host and the fetus have a right-of-way conflict that cannot be resolved without one losing. and for all this the conclusion is that the initial proposition is an incomplete logic loop.
