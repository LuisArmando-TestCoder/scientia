## argument_reasons

- Biological Essentialism: The claim that a specific DNA sequence constitutes the totality of a 'person's' moral value.
- Potentiality Argument: The assumption that the capacity to become a rational agent confers the status of a rational agent currently.
- Categorical Uniformity: The attempt to apply identical moral rights to entities at vastly different stages of neurological development.

## reconclusion

Moral personhood is a status assigned by a community based on shared traits, and while genetic identity is a necessary precursor for human personhood, it is demonstrably insufficient because it excludes the required emergent properties of consciousness and relational existence.
