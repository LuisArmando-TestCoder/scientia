## input_semantic_node

Fetal personhood from conception in the context of abortion ethics.

## central_semantic_node

Ontological status of the zygote/fetus as a bearer of full moral personhood rights.

## argument_reasons

- Biological continuity: Argument that no non-arbitrary line can be drawn between conception and adulthood.
- Potentiality: Argument that because the fetus will become a person, it already possesses the rights of a person.
- Genetic identity: The presence of a unique DNA code at conception as the marker of human individuality.
- Moral status parity: The claim that if X is a person, killing X is wrong, applied to the fetus.

## ontological_signature

- **nature**: Axiomatic moral claim
- **function**: To establish a legal and ethical prohibition of abortion.
- **domain**: Bioethics and Meta-ethics
- **form**: Linear causal progression (biological birth to moral rights)
- **tension**: Potentiality vs. Actuality: The tension between what a thing is and what it will be.
- **limit**: The dependency on a metaphysical definition of 'person' which is not biologically falsifiable.

## reconclusion

The proposition 'A fetus is a person from conception' is a foundational axiom within specific moral ontologies but lacks universal empirical validity because the property of 'personhood' is a sociolinguistic construct projected onto biological substrates, not a detectable physical property, and for all this, the question of abortion's moral justifiability remains a conflict between the right to bodily autonomy and the assigned value of potential life.

## preconceptual_reconclusion
