## central_semantic_node

Ontological and moral status of the fetus as a person from conception

## argument_reasons

- Identity Argument: Continuity of biological development suggests no non-arbitrary point of change from non-person to person.
- Genetic Argument: Possession of a unique human genome at zygote stage constitutes the sufficient condition for humanity.
- Potentiality Argument: The inherent capacity to become an adult human grants current personhood status or equivalent rights.
- Moral Rights Parity: If the fetus is a person, then the right to life is considered functionally equivalent to that of an adult.

## conclusion

The claim relies on bridging the 'is-ought' gap by equating a biological start point (conception) with a moral status (personhood), yet fail to account for the fact that personhood is a relational and functional designation requiring more than just cellular existence, and for all this the assertion that a fetus is a person from conception remains a metaphysical postulate rather than a logical necessity.
