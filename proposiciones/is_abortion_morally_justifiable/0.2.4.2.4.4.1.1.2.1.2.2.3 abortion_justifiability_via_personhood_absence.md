## causal_chain

Moral systems protect persons. Personhood is defined by specific traits. If a fetus lacks these traits, it is not a person. If it is not a person, it lacks the right to life. If it lacks the right to life, terminating the pregnancy does not violate a right. If no right is violated, the act is justifiable. However, the selection of traits is subjective. Therefore, the justifiability of abortion is as stable as the definition of personhood. Because the definition of personhood is unstable, the justifiability of abortion is a derivative of a fluctuating semantic consensus. This flux ensures that the argument remains logically valid but empirically uncollapsable.
