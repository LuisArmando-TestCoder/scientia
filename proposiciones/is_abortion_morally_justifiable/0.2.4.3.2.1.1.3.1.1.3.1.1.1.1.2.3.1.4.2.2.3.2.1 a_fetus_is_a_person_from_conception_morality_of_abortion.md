## conclusion

If a fetus is granted personhood at conception, it gains a right to life that mirrors an adult's, yet this right exist within a biological system where it competes with the adult's bodily autonomy; since rights between persons are typically balanced such that one cannot use another's body without consent (Thomson, 1971), and for all this, the statement 'a fetus is a person' remains an uncollapsable ontological claim that serves as a premise for moral restriction but fails to resolve the conflict of competing rights between two persons in one body.
