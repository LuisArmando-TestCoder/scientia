## central_semantic_node

Personhood-Dependent Moral Justifiability

## argument_reasons

- The inherent rights of an entity are traditionally derived from its status as a 'person' within a legal or moral framework.
- The definition of 'justifiable' requires a conflict of rights where one set of interests (autonomy) is weighed against another (life).
- Absence of a universal biological or metaphysical consensus on the 'start' of personhood creates a logical dependency on the definition chosen.

## ontological_signature

- **nature**: Axiomatic Dependency
- **function**: To establish a qualifying threshold for the application of moral rights.
- **domain**: Ethics, Jurisprudence, Ontological Biology.
- **form**: Conditional Syllogism.
- **tension**: The criteria for personhood (consciousness vs. genetic potential) are often selected to suit the desired moral conclusion.
- **limit**: Restricted by the subjective nature of 'value' assigned to biological milestones.

## conclusion

If personhood is defined by cognitive capacity, abortion is justifiable early in pregnancy, yet if defined by the Thompson override, personhood is a secondary variable, and for all this, the original proposition is an incomplete logical constraint that fails to account for the dual-subject nature of the medical event.

## reconclusion

The dependency on personhood is a semantic bottleneck that masks a deeper conflict between two fundamental 'goods': life and liberty; because personhood is an assigned status rather than a discovered one, the proposition remains an undecidable node within a static logic system.

## preconceptual_reconclusion

It's like trying to decide when a pile of sand becomes a 'mountain' just so you can decide if it's okay to move it; we are just arguing about names to make ourselves feel better about our choices.

## causal_chain

Human societies grant rights based on the recognized category of personhood—abortion involves an entity whose membership in this category is disputed based on varying physiological milestones—if personhood is the sole anchor then morality is a slave to a definition—definitions vary by culture and philosophy leading to undefined truth states—bodily autonomy provides a secondary causal path where justifiability exists regardless of the status of the second entity—therefore the single-node dependency on personhood is a logical reduction that fails to resolve the causal tension between two competing rights-holders in a shared biological space.
