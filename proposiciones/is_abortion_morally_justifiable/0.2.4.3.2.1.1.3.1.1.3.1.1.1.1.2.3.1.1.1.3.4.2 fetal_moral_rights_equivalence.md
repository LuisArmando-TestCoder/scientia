Ontological and Moral Parity of Fetuses and Adults

## argument_reasons

- Identity Continuity: The argument assumes that because a fetus becomes an adult, it must possess the same rights throughout the process.
- Potentiality vs Actuality: It equates the potential to exercise rights with the current possession of those rights.
- Biological Essentialism: Species membership (Homo sapiens) is used as the sole criterion for personhood, bypassing functional or cognitive requirements.

- **tension**: Static rights vs. dynamic biological development.
- **limit**: Dependent on the subjective definition of the start of personhood.
