## conclusion

If fetal personhood is granted as an axiom, the logic shifts to property and autonomy rights; given that modern legal systems (e.g., McFall v. Abortion is justifiable despite personhood.

## reconclusion

The moral justifiability of abortion under the premise of fetal personhood cannot be resolved through personhood alone; it requires a hierarchy of rights where bodily integrity is either absolute or subordinate to the preservation of life. Empirically, the 'right to life' never includes the 'right to be sustained by another's body' in any other legal context, which makes abortion justifiable as a withdrawal of biological support, not necessarily a denial of personhood.
