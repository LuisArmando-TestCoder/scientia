## conclusion

If personhood is defined by genetic uniqueness, then a fetus is a person, but because personhood historically and legally requires sentient capacity or birth, the claim remains an uncollapsible axiom; and for all this, the moral justifiability of abortion cannot be settled by biological facts alone but requires a choice between competing primary rights. Abortion's morality is an emergent property of the weight given to potentiality vs. autonomy.
