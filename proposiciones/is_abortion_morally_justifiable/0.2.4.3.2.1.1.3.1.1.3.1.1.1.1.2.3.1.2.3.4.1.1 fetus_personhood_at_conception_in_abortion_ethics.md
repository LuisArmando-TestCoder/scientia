## reconclusion

The proposition 'a fetus is a person from conception' is a metaphysical postulate that serves as a foundation for a specific moral architecture; however, because personhood is not a detectable physical property, the statement cannot be validated through first principles alone without first assuming a specific ontological definition of 'the self'.

## preconceptual_reconclusion

Since we can't see 'person-ness' under a microscope, we are just arguing about when we decide to start being kind to a new life.

## causal_chain

Biological life begins at fertilization because a unique DNA sequence is formed. Genetic uniqueness is often equated with individual personhood in certain moral frameworks. If a fetus is a person, it inherits a right to life equivalent to that of an adult. This right to life is then positioned as an absolute barrier against any action that would terminate the biological process. This creates a causal conflict with the pregnant person's right to bodily self-determination. The resolution depends entirely on which entity is prioritized as the primary subject of rights. Because there is no objective biological test for the moment a 'person' emerges distinct from 'life', the chain remains logically valid but epistemologically unmoored, leading to the necessary conclusion that the morality of abortion is a byproduct of the chosen axiom regarding the start of personhood.
