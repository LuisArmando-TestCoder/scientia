## causal_chain

Abortion involves terminating a biological process. The morality of this act hinges on 'personhood.' Personhood is often defined by sentience because without the ability to suffer or experience, there is no subject to whom harm can be done. Sentience requires a functioning nervous system. Science shows this system develops over time and is not present at the start. Therefore, early-stage termination lacks a sentient victim. Without a sentient victim, the act cannot be characterized as murder under a sentience-based framework. Thus, if the sentience-personhood link is accepted, early abortion is logically justifiable. This justifies the initial exploration of sentience as the key variable in the moral equation.
