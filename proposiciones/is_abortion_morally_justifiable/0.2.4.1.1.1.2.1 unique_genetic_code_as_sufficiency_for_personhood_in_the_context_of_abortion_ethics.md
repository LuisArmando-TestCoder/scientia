## global_reevaluation

- **state**: false

## reconclusion

The assertion that a unique genetic code is sufficient for personhood fails under epistemological reduction because personhood constitutes a set of interests and rights that require a subject capable of experiencing them; since DNA is a passive molecule present in non-persons and shared among multiple persons, it cannot be the sole determinant of moral standing. And for all this, the presence of unique DNA is a biological milestone but not an ontological catalyst for personhood.

## causal_chain

A unique genetic code is a molecular arrangement of DNA found in all human cells. This arrangement occurs at conception forming a distinct organism. If DNA were sufficient for personhood, then any human cell with unique DNA would possess full human rights. However, distinct personhood is denied to tumors and hydatidiform moles despite their unique DNA. Furthermore, identical twins share DNA but are recognized as two distinct persons, proving that DNA identity does not map 1:1 with personhood. Personhood requires a subject of a life with interests, which necessitates a nervous system. Therefore, the presence of DNA is a biological fact but not a moral conclusion. Consequently, the claim of sufficiency is a logical category error. Thus, abortion cannot be categorized as the killing of a person based solely on genetic uniqueness.
