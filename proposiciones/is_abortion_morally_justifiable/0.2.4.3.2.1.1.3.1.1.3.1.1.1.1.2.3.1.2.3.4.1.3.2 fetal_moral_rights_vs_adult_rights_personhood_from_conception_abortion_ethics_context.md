## argument_reasons

- Biological continuity argument: a unique DNA profile is established at conception, creating a distinct human life.
- Potentiality argument: the fetus is a 'potential' adult and thus inherits rights teleologically.
- Absence of non-arbitrary milestones: any point after conception (viability, birth) is seen as a change in degree, not in fundamental nature.

## reconclusion

The proposition is a second-order value judgment masquerading as a first-order biological fact. Because morality is a system of interaction between conscious agents, assigning full rights to a non-sentient organism is a symbolic projection of value rather than a deduction of natural law.
