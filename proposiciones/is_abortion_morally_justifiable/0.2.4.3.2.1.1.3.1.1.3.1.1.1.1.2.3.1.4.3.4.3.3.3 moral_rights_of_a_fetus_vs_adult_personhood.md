## input_semantic_node

Moral rights of a fetus vs. adult personhood

## central_semantic_node

The ontological and moral equivalence of a fetus and an adult person

## argument_reasons

- Identity Continuity: The argument that a fetus is a person from conception because it possesses a unique genetic code and potential for adult life justification for the 'pro-life' stance.
- Moral Status Hierarchy: The argument that moral rights are contingent upon Sentience or Rationality, which a fetus lacks at early stages providing justification for the 'pro-choice' stance.
- Categorical Imperative: The claim that if a fetus is a person, then killing it is a violation of the right to life, regardless of the mother's autonomy.

## boolean_formula_of_the_argument

A === B && (B ? !C : undefined)

## why_the_formula_structure_is_incorrect

It assumes a direct causal link between personhood and the total lack of moral justification for abortion, ignoring the 'Good Samaritan' argument (autonomy).

## boolean_argument_reformulation

(A === B) && (B && !C) || (!B && C)

## boolean_formula_to_natural_language

If A (personhood) is equivalent to B (adult rights), then personhood implies the immorality of abortion, whereas lack of personhood implies its justification.

## conclusion

If we define personhood by DNA, abortion is murder; if we define it by consciousness, it is not; and for all this the moral status of the fetus is a 'computational irreducibility' because the premises are chosen, not discovered. Existence precedes essence.

## reconclusion

Moral rights are not inherent biological properties but are normative assignments; therefore, the equivalence of a fetus and an adult is a choice of first principles, not a biological fact, rendering the conflict a clash of competing axioms. and for all this the moral status is an uncollapsable superposition of potential and actual states.
