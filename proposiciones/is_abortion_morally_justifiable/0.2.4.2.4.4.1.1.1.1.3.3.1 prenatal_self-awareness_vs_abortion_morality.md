## conclusion

If moral personhood is predicated on the capacity for self-awareness, and self-awareness is a functional emergent property requiring specific neural architecture (thalamocortical complex) which only matures in the third trimester, then the proposition that personhood exists from conception is biologically false, making abortion prior to that threshold logically distinguishable from the termination of a person, and for all this the moral justifiability of abortion remains legally valid until the point of integrated cortical function.
