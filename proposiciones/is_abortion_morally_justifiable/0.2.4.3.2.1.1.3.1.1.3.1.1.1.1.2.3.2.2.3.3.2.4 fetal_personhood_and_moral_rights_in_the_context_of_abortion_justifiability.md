## input_semantic_node

## central_semantic_node

Equivalence of Moral Status between Fetus and Adult

## argument_reasons

- The 'Personhood Argument' assumes that if a fetus is a person, it automatically possesses a right to life that outweighs a woman’s right to bodily autonomy.
- The 'Ontological Continuity Argument' suggests that because there is no non-arbitrary point of development, rights must begin at conception.
- The 'Potentiality Argument' claims that because a fetus will become an adult, it should be treated with the same moral weight now.

## ontological_signature

- **nature**: Axiomatic moral claim
- **function**: To establish a legal and ethical prohibition of abortion by equalizing subjects.
- **domain**: Bioethics, Legal Philosophy, Ontology
- **form**: Hierarchical attribution of rights based on essence.
- **tension**: Collision between the right to life of the fetus and the bodily integrity of the gestating person.
- **limit**: Dependent on the pre-scientific or metaphysical definition of 'person' which is not empirically falsifiable.

## conclusion

## reconclusion

The claim that a fetus has adult moral rights is an ontological assertion that fails to resolve the ethics of abortion because a right to life never includes the right to occupy another human's body against their will; thus, the justification for abortion remains independent of fetal status. And for all this, the statement is a category error that confuses biological existence with moral obligation.
