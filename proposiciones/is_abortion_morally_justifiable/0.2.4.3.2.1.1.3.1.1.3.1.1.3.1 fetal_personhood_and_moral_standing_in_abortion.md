## logical_tension

- **useful_contradiction**: A fetus is biologically 'human' but may not yet be an 'individual person' in a psychological sense.

## global_reevaluation

## reconclusion

The assertion that a fetus is a person from conception is a metaphysical anchor used to resolve the ethical ambiguity of abortion by collapsing the spectrum of development into a binary state. While biologically continuous, the transition to personhood lacks a discrete physical threshold, making the premise an act of 'moral naming' rather than 'scientific discovery.'

## preconceptual_reconclusion

Because we can't see 'personhood' under a microscope, we argue about when the magic starts, but the answer depends on which set of rules you choose to follow before you even look.

## causal_chain

Conception initiates a unique genetic sequence which establishes biological humanity but not necessarily the cognitive agency associated with personhood. If personhood is granted at conception based on potential, then the fetus acquires a right to life. This right then enters into direct conflict with the pregnant individual's right to bodily self-determination. The resolution of this conflict is not found in biology but in the value-weighting of the two competing entities. Therefore, the statement is a normative claim that functions as a prerequisite for a specific legal outcome rather than a derivable observation from first principles of consciousness.
