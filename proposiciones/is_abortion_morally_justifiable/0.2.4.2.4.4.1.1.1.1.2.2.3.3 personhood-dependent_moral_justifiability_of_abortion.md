## causal_chain

Abortion involves a fetus within a pregnant person. Moral justifiability requires a reason for action. The prompt suggests personhood is that reason. Personhood grants a right to life. A right to life usually makes ending life wrong. However, the pregnant person has bodily autonomy. Autonomy grants the right to control one's body. If autonomy is stronger than the right to life of a dependent, abortion is justified regardless of personhood. If personhood is not defined, the right to life is not established. If the right to life is not established, the dependency on personhood fails to provide a verdict. Since personhood is a subjective philosophical construct, the dependency link is a chain attached to a cloud, leaving the final conclusion floating until a society or individual anchors it to a chosen starting rule.
