- Categorical Prohibition: If equivalence is established, abortion is categorized as the termination of a person's life, equal to homicide.

## reconclusion

The proposition that a fetus has equal moral rights to an adult is an axiomatic deduction from the premise of conception-based personhood; however, because personhood is not a physical property but a status assigned by a moral framework, the validity of the rights-claim is contingent upon the initial acceptance of that framework. Thus, the argument is structurally sound within its own logic but remains epistemologically unproven in a pluralistic moral field.
