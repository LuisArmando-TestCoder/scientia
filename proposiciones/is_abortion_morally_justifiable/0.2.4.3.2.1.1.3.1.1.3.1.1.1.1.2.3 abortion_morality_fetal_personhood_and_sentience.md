## conclusion

If morality is the management of suffering/flourishing and a fetus cannot suffer/flourish without a functional brain, then rights cannot be anchored in biological presence alone, and for all this, the granting of full moral rights to a non-sentient fetus is a logical contradiction.

## reconclusion

Moral rights are a function of the capacity to value one's existence; therefore, an entity that cannot value cannot possess rights, rendering the 'Personhood at Conception' claim relevant only to biological classification and not moral obligation until the emergence of subjective awareness.

## preconceptual_reconclusion

You can't hurt someone who isn't there yet, even if their body has started to grow.

## causal_chain

Rights exist to protect feelings and interests. Interests require a mind to hold them. A mind requires a working brain. A fetus at conception has no working brain. Therefore, a fetus at conception has no mind and no interests to protect. If rights are only for those with interests, then a fetus at conception cannot have rights. If it has no rights, then abortion cannot be a violation of rights. Thus, the justification for abortion hinges entirely on the timing of mental emergence rather than biological status.
