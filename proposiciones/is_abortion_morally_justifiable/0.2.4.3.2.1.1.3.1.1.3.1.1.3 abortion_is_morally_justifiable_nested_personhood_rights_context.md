## causal_chain

Determining if abortion is okay requires knowing if a fetus is a person because persons have a right to life that others must respect; personhood is usually linked to being human, but in a moral sense, it means having thoughts, feelings, or a future like ours; if we say personhood starts at the very beginning of a cell's growth, then the right to life starts there too, but this right then fights with the mother's right to control what happens inside her own skin; since science can tell us when a heart beats but not when a 'soul' or 'person' arrives, we are left with a choice between two starting points that lead to opposite answers; therefore, the answer is not found in a laboratory but in the rules we choose to live by as a group, which means the conclusion changes depending on which rule you put at the very top of your list.
