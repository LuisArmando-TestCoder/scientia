## causal_chain

Abortion is the termination of a biological process within a human body. Any biological process within a human body requires the ongoing consent of that body's owner. Consent is the exercise of moral agency. Moral agency is the highest form of personhood. Therefore, the agent (mother) has primary jurisdiction over the space regardless of the ontological status of the fetus. If jurisdiction is primary, then permission to terminate is a subset of that jurisdiction. If permission exists as a subset of jurisdiction, then justifiability is established. Because this justifiability is derived from the agent's jurisdiction rather than the subject's nature, the subject's personhood is irrelevant. Thus, justifiability exists independently of the personhood of the fetus.
