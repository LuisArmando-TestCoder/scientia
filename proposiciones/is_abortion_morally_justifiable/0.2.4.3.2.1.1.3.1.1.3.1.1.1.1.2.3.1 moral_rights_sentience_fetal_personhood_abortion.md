## causal_chain

A fetus is defined as a person at conception which triggers the assignment of full moral rights equivalent to an adult regardless of the biological absence of neuro-physiological structures required for sentience. Under the provided constraint that moral rights are *only* for sentient beings, a fetus would have no rights unless the definition of personhood is used to 'pre-load' sentience or bypass the requirement. This creates an internal logic crash because the fetus is simultaneously a holder of rights (as a person) and a non-holder of rights (as a non-sentient). If rights require sentience, and the fetus lacks it, abortion is justified; however, if personhood equals rights from the start, abortion is not justified. Therefore, the justification of abortion is not a fact to be found but a result of which rule—Sentience or Personhood—is placed at the top of the pile.
