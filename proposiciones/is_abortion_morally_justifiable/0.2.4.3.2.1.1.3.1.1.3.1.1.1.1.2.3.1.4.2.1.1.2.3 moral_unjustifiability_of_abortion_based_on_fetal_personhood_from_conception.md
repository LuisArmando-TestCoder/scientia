## conclusion

If we define a fetus as a person with absolute rights (Don Marquis, 'Why Abortion is Immoral'), then termination is an ontological violation; however, because personhood is an attributed status rather than a measurable physical property, and the 'Violinist' paradox demonstrates that rights to life don't equate to rights to use another's body, the absolute claim of 'always' fails internal logical consistency without total suppression of the mother's personhood; and for all this, the statement remains an unresolved logical dependency on an unproven axiom.

Because personhood itself is an undefined variable in the biological field, the entire argument functions as an axiomatic anchor rather than a logical proof.
