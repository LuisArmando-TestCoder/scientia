## causal_chain

Axiom of fertilization defines biological human life as the start of the entity. Social-legal frameworks translate biological human into 'person' to grant protections. If 'person' status is granted, 'Right to Life' (RTL) is activated. Traditional logic assumes RTL is an absolute positive right to exist. Jurisprudence and bodily autonomy ethics redefine RTL as a 'negative right' (not to be killed unjustly) rather than a 'positive right' to use another's organs. The conflict between RTL and Bodily Autonomy (BA) creates a hierarchy where BA is often recognized as the boundary of consent. Therefore, termination is the withdrawal of consent for use of the body, which is a justifiable act of autonomy even if the entity terminated has the standing of a person. Causal necessity dictates that no person can be a 'slave' to the survival of another, which justifies the act of abortion through the lens of self-sovereignty. This links the first principle of self-ownership to the specific conclusion that moral justification is independent of the fetal personhood axiom.
