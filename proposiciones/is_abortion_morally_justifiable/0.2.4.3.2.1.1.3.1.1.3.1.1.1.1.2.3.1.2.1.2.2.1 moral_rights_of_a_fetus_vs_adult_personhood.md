## conclusion

If we define personhood specifically as beginning at conception, then abortion becomes logically incompatible with that premise; however, because personhood is a philosophical assignment and not a biological fact, and because the right to life does not typically include the right to occupy another's body against their will (Judith Jarvis Thomson, 1971), and for all this, the statement of absolute moral parity remains an unproven axiomatic preference rather than a universal truth.
