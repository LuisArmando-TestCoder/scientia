## conclusion

If we define personhood as the presence of unique biological human life, then a fetus is a person from conception; if we define personhood as the presence of sentient consciousness, then it is not; and for all this biological continuity suggests that while life is a spectrum, the assignment of 'personhood' remains an axiomatic choice that cannot be resolved by biology alone, rendering the moral justifiability of abortion a conflict of irreconcilable foundational premises. and for all this, the status of the fetus is a social-metaphysical designation rather than a biological certainty.
