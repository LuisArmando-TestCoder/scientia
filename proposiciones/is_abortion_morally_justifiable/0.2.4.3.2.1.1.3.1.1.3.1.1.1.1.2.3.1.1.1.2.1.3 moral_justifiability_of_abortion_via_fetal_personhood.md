## causal_chain

Determining moral justifiability requires defining the moral status of the participants. Defining a fetus as a person from conception creates a maximum right-to-life claim. This claim is nested within the question itself to create a logical constraint. Such a constraint requires that any act ending the life must be viewed through the lens of taking a person's life. This creates a conflict with the mother's bodily autonomy. Bodily autonomy is also a person-level right. Therefore the justifiability of abortion depends on which person's rights are prioritized when two people exist in a state of physical dependency. This dependency is unique to gestation. Unique states cannot be solved by general rules. Thus the circular definition of personhood at conception serves to bypass the physical reality of dependency by treating the fetus as an abstract adult. This abstraction fails to acknowledge the biological necessity of the mother's agency. Therefore the conclusion is perpetually deferred unless the axiom of personhood is either collapsed or qualified by the context of dependency.
