## argument_reasons

- Utilitarian balance: Weighing the suffering of the pregnant individual against the cessation of a potential life.

## logical_tension

- **paradox**: The 'Dependency Paradox': The fetus's 'right' to life is entirely dependent on the biological subvention of the very person whose rights it would supposedly restrict.
- **ambiguity**: The term 'human' vs. 'person'—one is biological, the other is a social/moral status.
- **useful_contradiction**: A fetus is simultaneously 'part' of the pregnant person (biologically) and 'not-part' (genetically/potentially).

Moral justification for abortion is logically valid when predicated on the axiom of bodily autonomy as the foundational human right; however, it remains a contested proposition in frameworks that prioritize the 'potentiality of being' as an equivalent or superior moral value, resulting in a systemic ethical stalemate that can only be resolved through political or legal imposition rather than pure logical deduction.
