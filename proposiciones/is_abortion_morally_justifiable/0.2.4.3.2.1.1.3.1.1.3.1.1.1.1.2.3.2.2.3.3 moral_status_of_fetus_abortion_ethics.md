- Personhood is claimed to begin at conception (biological start point).
- Equivalence between fetus and adult implies equal right to life and bodily autonomy considerations.
- The justification for abortion depends on whether fetal rights override or are overridden by maternal rights.

## boolean_formula_to_natural_language

## conclusion

If personhood is defined by the presence of a unique genetic code, then A is true, which implies B is true, leading to a conflict where the fetus's right to life is positioned against the adult's bodily integrity; however, since personhood is functionally linked to sentience in most biological frameworks (e.g., Giubilini & Minerva, 2012), the identity of A remains empirically uncollapsable, and for all this, the statement remains an unresolved tension between the potentiality of the fetus and the actuality of the adult.
