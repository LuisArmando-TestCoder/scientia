## conclusion

If we define personhood as beginning at the moment of genetic completion, then a fetus is a person; if a fetus is a person, it logically commands the same protection as any other person; however, since personhood is an unprovable metaphysical label and biological life exists on a continuum, the equivalence remains a subjective axiom rather than an empirical fact, and for all this fetal rights equal adult rights remains an uncollapsible proposition in a pluralistic society.
