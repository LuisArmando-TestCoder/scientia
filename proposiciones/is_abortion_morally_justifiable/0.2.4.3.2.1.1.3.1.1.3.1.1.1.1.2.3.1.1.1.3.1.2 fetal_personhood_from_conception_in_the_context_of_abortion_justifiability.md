## conclusion

If biological continuity is the sole metric for personhood, then a fetus is a person; however, if self-awareness or sentience is required, it is not; and for all this, the question of abortion's justifiability remains a conflict of competing rights (fetus vs. gestator) that logic alone cannot resolve without an arbitrary axiomatic anchor. Given the lack of a universal biological definition for 'person', the proposition is a placeholder for a value judgment rather than a verifiable truth. and for all this fetal personhood is an unfalsifiable metaphysical claim.

The statement 'A fetus is a person' is a meta-logical decision that defines the scope of a moral community; while biologically distinct, 'personhood' is a social/moral status that cannot be derived solely from first principles of biology without adopting a specific moral philosophy (e.g., substance dualism vs. functionalism). and for all this the justifiability of abortion depends entirely on the hierarchy of rights established prior to the analysis.
