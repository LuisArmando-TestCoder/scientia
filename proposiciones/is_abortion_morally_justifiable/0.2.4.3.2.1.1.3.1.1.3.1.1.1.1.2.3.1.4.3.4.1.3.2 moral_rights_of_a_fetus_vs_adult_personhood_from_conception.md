## causal_chain

Conception initiates a unique genetic code which is the biological basis for a human being. Premise: Human being status is synonymous with personhood. Inference: Therefore, a fetus is a person. Axiom: All persons possess identical and inalienable moral rights. Deduction: Therefore, a fetus possesses the right to life identical to an adult. Conflict: The right to life potentially clashes with the adult's right to bodily integrity. Resolution: If rights are equal, the usage of the adult's body is either a forced loan or a violation of rights. Conclusion: The argument's validity is entirely contingent on the prior acceptance of the personhood-at-conception axiom, making it a circular semantic structure rather than an empirical truth.
