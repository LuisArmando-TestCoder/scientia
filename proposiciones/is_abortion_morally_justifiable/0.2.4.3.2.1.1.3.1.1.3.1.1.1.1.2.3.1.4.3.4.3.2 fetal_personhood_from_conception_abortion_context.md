## central_semantic_node

Ontological Personhood and Moral Equivalence of Fetuses

## argument_reasons

- Biological continuity: The zygote is a distinct human organism from fertilization, used to justify moral continuity.
- Potentiality argument: The capacity to become a rational agent is viewed as a current property of the entity.
- Moral rights claim: Equating biological human life with 'personhood' to invoke legal and ethical protections.

## ontological_signature

- **nature**: Axiomatic normative claim
- **function**: To establish a legal and moral bar for the prohibition of certain medical procedures.
- **domain**: Bioethics, Legal Ontology, Metaphysics
- **form**: Hierarchical extension (Biological -> Moral -> Legal)
- **tension**: Distinction between 'human life' (biological) and 'person' (philosophical/legal unit of account).
- **limit**: Dependent on the definition of 'person', which is not empirically observable but socially/metaphysically constructed.

## emerging_theory_or_intuition

Personhood is a 'cluster concept' involving biological, psychological, and social markers. Treating it as a binary 'on/off' switch at conception is a categorical simplification used for legal utility rather than ontological accuracy.

## logical_tension

- **paradox**: The Potentiality Paradox: Treating a potential something as the actual something.
- **ambiguity**: The shift between 'human' (species) and 'person' (moral status).
- **useful_contradiction**: A fetus is both 'part' of the pregnant person (biologically dependent) and 'distinct' (genetically unique).

## analogous_reorganization

- The 'Ship of Theseus': When does a collection of parts become a specific identity?
- Acorn vs. Oak: The biological template versus the manifested form.

## implications

- Legislation based on this premise often requires ignoring the competing personhood/rights of the pregnant individual.
- If accepted, it necessitates a total re-evaluation of IVF, stem cell research, and reproductive technology.

## global_reevaluation

- **state**: self-referential
- **criteria**: The argument defines its own terms (personhood = human life) to force its own conclusion, creating a circular loop.

## reconclusion

The assertion that a fetus is a person from conception is a metaphysical axiom designed to settle a value conflict by masquerading as a biological fact; until the definition of personhood is empirically grounded (likely impossible), the claim remains a matter of chosen moral framework rather than external verification.
