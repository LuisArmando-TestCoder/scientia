## conclusion

If personhood is defined by biological potential then the fetus is a person, if personhood is defined by cognitive function then it is not, and since moral rights are derived from a social contract the fetus cannot participate in, any absolute claim to its personhood is a metaphysical axiom rather than a biological fact; and for all this, the question of abortion's justifiability remains a conflict of irreconcilable primary values regarding the definition of a human life's beginning. The morality of the act is predicated on a self-referential loop of definitions.
