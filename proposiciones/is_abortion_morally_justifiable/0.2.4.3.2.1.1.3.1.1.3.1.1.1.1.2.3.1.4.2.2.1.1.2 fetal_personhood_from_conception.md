Ontological and Moral Status of the Zygote/Fetus

## argument_reasons

- Biological Continuity: The zygote is a distinct, living human organism from the moment of fertilization, suggesting a continuous developmental path to adulthood.
- Potentiality: The fetus possesses the inherent capacity to develop into a being with manifest personhood, which is often argued to confer moral value prematurely.
- Genetic Uniqueness: The possession of a unique genetic code distinct from the parents is used as a marker for a new individual entity.
- Moral Equalitarianism: The claim that rights are inherent to the species rather than dependent on developmental milestones.

## why_the_formula_structure_is_incorrect

The formula assumes that personhood (A) and equal rights (B) automatically negate moral justification (C), but it fails to account for the Independent Variable of Bodily Autonomy (D), which can make C true even if A and B are true.

## boolean_argument_reformulation

## boolean_formula_to_natural_language

## conclusion

If the status of personhood is a socially assigned role rather than a biological fact, and if bodily autonomy remains a primary right in all other legal contexts, then the claim of personhood from conception is a categorical error that conflates 'human DNA' with 'civic personhood', and for all this, the morality of abortion cannot be invalidated by the personhood argument alone because the right to life does not grant a right to non-consensual use of another's biological systems.

## logical_tension

- **paradox**: The Potentiality Paradox: Treating a potential person as an actual person grants rights to a being that cannot yet exercise them, at the expense of a being currently exercising them.
- **ambiguity**: The ambiguity of the 'Soul' or 'Consciousness' as a marker of personhood.
- **useful_contradiction**: A fetus is both 'part of' the pregnant person (biologically) and 'distinct from' them (genetically), creating a dual-state entity.
