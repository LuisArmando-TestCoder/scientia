## conclusion

If moral status is contingent upon the capacity for sentience as the primary axiom, then the biological event of conception—which lacks neural substrates—cannot logically confer personhood or adult-equivalent rights, and for all this, the assertion of equal moral standing for a pre-sentient fetus is a logical contradiction. and for all this the belief in equivalent rights from conception is functionally incompatible with a sentience-based ethical framework.
