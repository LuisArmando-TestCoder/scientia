## causal_chain

Conception creates a unique genetic signature which establishes biological life. Some moral systems define this signature as the necessary and sufficient condition for personhood which grants the entity full moral rights. If a fetus has full moral rights, its right to life is considered equal to that of an adult. In a conflict of rights, the right to life is usually prioritized over the right to bodily autonomy within these systems. Therefore, if the first premise of conception-personhood is accepted, the causal chain terminates in the conclusion that abortion is never morally justifiable. However, if the first premise is viewed as a developmental process rather than a point-in-time event, the chain breaks at the definition of 'person', allowing for different moral outcomes regarding the justifiability of abortion based on the competing rights of the pregnant individual. The entire structure is thus a feedback loop where the desired endpoint dictates the entry-point axiom.
