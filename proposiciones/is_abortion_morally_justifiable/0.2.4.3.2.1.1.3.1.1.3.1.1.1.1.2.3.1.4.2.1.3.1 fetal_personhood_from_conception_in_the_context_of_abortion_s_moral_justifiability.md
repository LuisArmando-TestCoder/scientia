## argument_reasons

- The assertion of personhood at conception serves as a non-arbitrary biological starting point for legal and moral protection.
- Potentiality argument: the zygote contains the entire genetic blueprint of a human being, making it a person in development.
- Moral rights equivalency: if a fetus is a person, it possesses an inalienable right to life that competes with or supersedes bodily autonomy.

## conclusion

If personhood is defined by genetic uniqueness, then the fetus is a person; if personhood requires consciousness, it is not; however, the moral justifiability of abortion depends on the hierarchy of conflicting rights regardless of the personhood status, and for all this, the statement remains a metaphysical axiom rather than an empirical fact.
