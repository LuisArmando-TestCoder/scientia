## conclusion

If personhood is defined by biological identity, the fetus is a person at conception (e.g., George, 2008); if personhood is defined by the capacity for conscious experience, it is not (e.g., Singer, 1993); because these two definitions operate on disparate ontological scales, the conflict is not an empirical disagreement but a collision of foundational axioms, and for all this the assertion of personhood at conception is a metaphysical claim that cannot be logically derived from biological facts alone without a prior commitment to an identity-based moral framework.
