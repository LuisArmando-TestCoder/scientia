- Bodily Autonomy: The moral justification for abortion often rests on the rights of the pregnant person, regardless of the embryo's status.

## boolean_formula_to_natural_language

## conclusion

If 'personhood' is defined by cognitive/sentient capacity, the embryo is not a person, and if 'personhood' is defined by genetic potential, it is; however, since moral rights are typically derived from the capacity to suffer or hold interests, the absence of these in an embryo suggests its status is secondary to the established rights of the pregnant individual, and for all this, abortion is morally justifiable as the protection of an actual person's autonomy over a potential person's development.
