## reconclusion

The rights of a fetus cannot be 'identical' to an adult's because rights are fundamentally linked to the capacity for interests, duties, and social interaction, which do not exist in the same way for a pre-sentient organism as they do for a conscious agent.

## preconceptual_reconclusion

Even if we say they are both people, they are in very different places; one is inside someone else and needs them to live, and the other is out in the world, so they cannot have the exact same rules.

## causal_chain

Conception initiates a unique biological life sequence which some philosophies equate to personhood while others require sentience or birth for such status and because rights are legal tools to protect autonomy which a fetus lacks due to total biological dependency the claim of identical rights fails to account for the unique moral conflict of the host's body and therefore rights must be seen as developmental or tiered rather than binary and fixed at the moment of fertilization.
