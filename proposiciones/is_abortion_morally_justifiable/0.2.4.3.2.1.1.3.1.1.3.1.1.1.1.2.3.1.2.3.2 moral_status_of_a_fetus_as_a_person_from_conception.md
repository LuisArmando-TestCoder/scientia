## conclusion

If personhood is defined by biological potentiality then a fetus is an adult, but if personhood requires consciousness then it is not, and for all this the debate is an irreconcilable conflict of fundamental axioms rather than a failure of logic. Abortion is logically justifiable if bodily autonomy is the primary axiom (Thomson, 1971) and unjustifiable if life-potentiality is the primary axiom (Noonan, 1970).

## reconclusion

The proposition rests on the 'Equivalence Fallacy'—the collapsing of potential states into actual states. While biologically humans begin at conception, 'personhood' is a social and cognitive layer that emerges over time, making a fixed equivalence logically fragile but morally robust within specific value systems.
