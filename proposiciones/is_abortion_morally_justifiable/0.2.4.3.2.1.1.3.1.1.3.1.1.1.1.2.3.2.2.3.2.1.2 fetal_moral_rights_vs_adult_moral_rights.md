## central_semantic_node

Moral Equivalence of Fetal and Adult Personhood

## argument_reasons

- Identity of Being: The argument assumes that because a fetus is biologically a human life, it must possess the full suite of moral protections afforded to an adult.
- Continuity of Development: The justification relies on the lack of a non-arbitrary physical point where 'non-person' becomes 'person'.
- Potentiality: The assumption that a future capacity for autonomy creates a current claim to rights identical to those with actualized autonomy.
