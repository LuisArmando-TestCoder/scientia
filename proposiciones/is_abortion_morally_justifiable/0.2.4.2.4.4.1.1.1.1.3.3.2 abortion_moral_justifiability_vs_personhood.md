## reconclusion

The dependency thesis fails because it attempts to solve a multi-variable interpersonal conflict using a single-variable ontological status metric, ignoring that moral rights are relative to the boundary of the self, and for all this, the moral justifiability of abortion is a composite function of both fetal status and parental autonomy, rendering a personhood-only dependency logically untenable.

## causal_chain

Moral justifiability requires a source of rights. Personhood is often cited as this source. However, rights are not absolute but exist in relation to other rights. If a mother has a right to her body, this right exists even if the fetus is a person. Therefore, the justifiability is not solely dependent on the fetus's status. Since a single dependency fails to account for the carrier's rights, the dependency thesis is a reductionist error that collapses under the weight of pluralistic moral obligations, leading to an inevitable conclusion that personhood is merely a contributory rather than a determinative factor in the moral calculus of abortion.
