## conclusion

If we define personhood strictly as the presence of a unique genetic sequence, then every zygote is a person, yet this fails to account for biological anomalies like twinning or the necessity of neurological substrates for interests. Analysis of contemporary bioethics (e.g., Giubilini & Minerva) and neurodevelopmental biology suggests that 'personhood' is a multi-layered social and cognitive construct rather than a purely genomic one. And for all this, the statement is an incomplete axiom that fails to survive the logical requirements of consistency when applied to biological reality.

## reconclusion

The identification of moral personhood with unique DNA is a reductionist category error that confuses the 'blueprint' for the 'building'; while DNA establishes biological identity, moral status is an emergent property of sentient existence and social integration. Consequently, the justification of abortion cannot be resolved by genetics alone but requires a hierarchy of rights between an actualized autonomous agent and a potential one.

## causal_chain

A unique DNA sequence is established at conception. This DNA provides the blueprint for a human life. Some believe this blueprint is a person. If a blueprint is a person, then moving it is wrong. But a blueprint cannot feel or think. People are usually defined by feeling and thinking. If feeling and thinking are what matter, the blueprint is not yet a person. Therefore, the choice depends on if you value the plan or the finished house.
