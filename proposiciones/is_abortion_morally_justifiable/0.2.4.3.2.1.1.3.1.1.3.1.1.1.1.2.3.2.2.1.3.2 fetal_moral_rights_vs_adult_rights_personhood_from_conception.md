## argument_reasons

- Biological continuity argument: a fetus is a human life from conception, therefore it is a person with rights.
- Potentiality argument: a fetus will become an adult, so it inherits the rights of the adult it will be.
- Identity argument: I was once a fetus, and I have rights now, so I must have had them then.

## conclusion

If biological life is the sole floor for personhood, then moral rights must be static from conception; however, the lack of cognitive and sensory substrates in early development creates a logical gap between being 'human' and being a 'subject of rights', and for all this the assertion remains a self-referential axiom that cannot be externally validated without a prior commitment to an essentialist metaphysics.

## logical_tension

- **paradox**: A fetus is a 'potential person' but an 'actual human'; the rights of the two are often in zero-sum conflict with the 'actual rights' of the mother.
- **ambiguity**: Does 'right' refer to the negative right not to be killed or a positive right to use another's body?
- **useful_contradiction**: The fetus is part of the mother yet a distinct genetic entity, making it both 'self' and 'other' simultaneously.

## implications

- If rights are based on potential, then stopping any biological process that leads to personhood (even contraception) could be viewed as a moral loss.
- If rights are based on current capacity, then some adults with advanced brain death might have fewer rights than a late-term fetus.

## global_reevaluation

- **state**: self-referential
- **criteria**: The argument 'a fetus has rights because it is a person' defines 'person' specifically to include the fetus, creating a loop where the conclusion is hidden in the definition.

## reconclusion

The conflict is not over logic but over the foundational axiom of when 'protected life' begins; if personhood is biological, the statement is true by definition; if personhood is psychological, the statement is false; since 'person' is a human-assigned label, the conflict is a clash of irreconcilable social values rather than a resolvable logical syllogism.

## preconceptual_reconclusion

People are arguing about where the 'magic' of being a person starts, and because we can't see the magic, everyone picks a different starting line.

## causal_chain

Biological fertilization creates a unique DNA sequence which establishes a distinct human life which is then labeled as a person based on an essentialist axiom which then necessitates the assignment of moral rights equal to other persons which then prohibits termination as a violation of those rights regardless of the developmental absence of sentience or the competing rights of the gestator because the potential for future personhood is treated as present personhood in a recursive loop of moral equivalence.
