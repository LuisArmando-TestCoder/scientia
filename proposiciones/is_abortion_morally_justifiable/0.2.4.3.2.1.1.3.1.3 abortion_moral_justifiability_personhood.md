## conclusion

If personhood is an arbitrary social marker and bodily autonomy is a fundamental right, then the restriction of abortion lacks a foundational moral imperative; however, if personhood is intrinsic from conception, the act remains a conflict of rights where the outcome is culturally/legally determined rather than logically settled, and for all this, the justifiability remains a superposition of conflicting rights that cannot be collapsed without picking an unprovable starting axiom.
