## conclusion

If biological continuity is the sole metric for personhood, then the zygote is a person, but because personhood in diverse ethical systems requires psychological or social markers not present at conception, the assertion remains an uncollapsed subjective axiom; and for all this, the morality of abortion cannot be irrefutably settled by first principles alone without a prior, arbitrary consensus on the start-point of the 'person' label.
