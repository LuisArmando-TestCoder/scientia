## causal_chain

Abortion requires a moral justification because it involves the cessation of biological life. If we assume that only 'persons' have a right to life, then the moral status of the act depends entirely on whether the entity is a person. If we define personhood through cognitive markers like consciousness or feeling, and these markers are absent in the early stages of a fetus, then the right to life does not yet exist for that entity. However, because personhood is a social construction and not a fixed biological point, different people pick different start dates, which means the justification 'floats' based on whose definition you use, leading to a loop where the act is justified only if you choose a definition that makes it justified.
