## causal_chain

Determining if abortion is justifiable requires defining the status of the fetus. The argument assumes a fetus is a person to grant it rights. These rights are then used to argue against abortion. However, the definition of personhood used is chosen specifically to prevent abortion. This creates a loop where the conclusion is hidden in the first word. Because personhood is not a physical part of the fetus like a limb but a name we give it, the truth of the statement depends on who is giving the name. Therefore, the logic is trapped in a circle where the answer is decided before the question is even asked.
