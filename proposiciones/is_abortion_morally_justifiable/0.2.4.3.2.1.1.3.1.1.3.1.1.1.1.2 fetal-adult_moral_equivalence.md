## central_semantic_node

Ontological Parity of Fetal and Adult Moral Rights

## argument_reasons

- Biological Continuity Argument: Life begins at conception, thus rights are inherent to the biological organism throughout its development.
- Potentiality Argument: The fetus possesses the potential to become a full person, and this potential confers current rights.
- Sentience/Cognition Argument: Rights are contingent on specific capacities (consciousness, pain, self-awareness) which are absent in early fetuses but present in adults.
- Personhood Argument: Legal and moral 'personhood' is a Social/Relational construct distinct from mere biological life.
