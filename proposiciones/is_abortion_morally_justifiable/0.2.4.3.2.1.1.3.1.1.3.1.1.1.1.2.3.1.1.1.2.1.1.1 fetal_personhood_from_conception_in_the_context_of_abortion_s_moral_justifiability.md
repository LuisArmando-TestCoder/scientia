## conclusion

If we define personhood strictly by biological continuity from the zygote stage, then rights must be applied uniformly; however, because personhood is a social construct based on cognitive capacity and social presence as evidenced by works like Peter Singer’s ‘Practical Ethics’, the premise 'A' remains an unproven axiom, and for all this the conclusion is that the moral status of the fetus is a progressive value rather than an instant absolute.
