## conclusion

If personhood is defined by biological onset (A) and rights are linked to personhood (B), then rights exist from conception, yet real-world applications (Thompson's Violinist) suggest that equal rights do not equate to a right to use another's body, and for all this the assertion of identical moral rights is an insufficient premise to render abortion inherently unjustifiable.

## reconclusion

The proposition that a fetus has identical moral rights to an adult acts as a theological or categorical axiom; however, because rights are relational and often contingent upon bodily autonomy, the absolute equivalence fails to provide an irrefutable path to the moral unjustifiability of abortion, as it ignores the necessary hierarchy of rights involved in physical gestation.
