## input_semantic_node

Moral status and personhood in abortion

## central_semantic_node

Personhood-dependent moral ontology

## argument_reasons

- Premise 1: Moral rights are exclusively derived from the status of and definition of personhood.
- Premise 2: A fetus lacks the necessary criteria (consciousness, self-awareness, etc.) to be defined as a person.
- Logical Bridge: If rights require personhood and the subject lacks personhood, rights are non-existent.
- Conclusion: Under these constraints, the act is justifiable due to the absence of a rights-bearing entity.

## logical_tension

- **paradox**: To define a person, a 'person' must first exist to create the definition, making the source of rights self-referential.
- **ambiguity**: The threshold of 'presence' (at what second does a set of neurons become a person?).
- **useful_contradiction**: A fetus is 'human' (biological) but not a 'person' (legal/moral), creating a split between nature and law.

## analogous_reorganization

- A corporate entity: it has legal rights only because we define it as a 'person' for specific functional reasons.

## implications

- Moral status is earned/attained rather than inherent.
