## input_semantic_node

Fetal Personhood & Abortion Morality

## central_semantic_node

Ontological Status of the Fetus within Moral Agency

## argument_reasons

- Biological Continuity: The argument that life begins at conception due to a unique genetic code.
- Potentiality: The claim that a fetus is a 'potential person' and thus possesses the rights of an actual person.
- Sentience: The argument that personhood requires a degree of consciousness or the ability to feel pain, which develops later in gestation.
- Bodily Autonomy: The counter-argument that even if personhood is granted, the moral right to self-determination of the pregnant individual supersedes the fetal right to use the host's body.

## boolean_formula_of_the_argument

## why_the_formula_structure_is_incorrect

The formula assumes personhood (C) is a direct output of DNA (A+B), but it ignores the variable of Bodily Autonomy (E), which dictates moral justifiability regardless of C.

## boolean_argument_reformulation

(!E || !C) === Moral_Justification

## boolean_formula_to_natural_language

If the host chooses to exercise bodily autonomy (E) or if the fetus is not a person (C), then that implies the statement 'abortion is morally justifiable' is true.

## conclusion

If personhood is an assigned social status rather than a biological fact, and if bodily autonomy is an absolute right of an existing person, then the dependence of abortion's morality on the 'personhood' of a fetus is a category error, and for all this the moral justifiability of abortion remains a conflict of rights rather than a definition of life.
