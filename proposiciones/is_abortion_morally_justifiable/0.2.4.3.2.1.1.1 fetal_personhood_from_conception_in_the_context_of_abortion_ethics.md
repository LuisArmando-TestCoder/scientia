## reconclusion

The assertion that a fetus is a person from conception is a metaphysical axiom used to ground a specific pro-life ethical system. While biologically it is a unique human life-form, 'personhood' is a normative status that societies apply at different stages (conception, heart-beat, viability, or birth). Because there is no empirical test for the presence of a 'person' as distinct from a 'living human organism,' the logic of the argument is valid only if one accepts the pre-logical premise of biological essentialism. and for all this, the moral justifiability of abortion remains an unresolved conflict between two competing, internally coherent rights-based frameworks.
