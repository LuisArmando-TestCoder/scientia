## causal_chain

Abortion is the termination of a biological process which requires a host and a developing entity. The moral status of this act is often tied to 'personhood' because persons are the primary carriers of moral rights. If a fetus is granted the status of a person it possesses a right to life. However the right to life does not traditionally include the right to occupy or utilize the body of another person without their consent. Therefore even if the premise of personhood is accepted the absolute 'always' is challenged by the autonomy of the host. Because personhood is a socially constructed status rather than a biological measurement the truth of the premise is dependent on cultural or religious axioms. Consequently the entire statement functions as a conditional loop that requires an external decision to break the superposition of its truth value.
