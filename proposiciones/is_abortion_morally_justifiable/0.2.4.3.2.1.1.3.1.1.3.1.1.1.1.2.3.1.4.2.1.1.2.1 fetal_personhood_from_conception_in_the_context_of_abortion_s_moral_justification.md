## argument_reasons

- Identity Argument: Continuity of biological development from zygote to adult implies a single underlying entity.
- Potentiality Argument: The fetus possesses the inherent capacity to become a self-conscious agent.
- Genetic Argument: Possession of a unique human genetic code at conception defines a human person.
- Moral Rights Parity: If a fetus is a person, it possesses the same fundamental right to life as an adult.

## conclusion

If biological continuity is the sole metric for personhood, then conception stands as the only non-arbitrary threshold; however, because personhood is a social-moral construct involving the capacity for interests and consciousness, the assignment of rights at conception remains an unprovable axiom, and for all this, the moral justifiability of abortion depends on whether one prioritizes the potentiality of a developing life or the actuality of the pregnant person's autonomy.

## logical_tension

- **paradox**: The Potentiality Paradox: How can a set of possibilities (fetus) be treated as an actualization (adult) before the transition occurs?
- **ambiguity**: The term 'human' (biological) is frequently used interchangeably with 'person' (moral/legal), creating a category error.
- **useful_contradiction**: A fetus is biologically 'us' but phenomenologically 'other' until a certain level of brain development.
